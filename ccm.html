Consciousness
Component Model (CCM)

The Consciousness Component Model (CCM) is the cumulative capability hierarchy within Consciousness Mechanics (CM). It specifies bounded control mechanisms as layered components (CL1…CLn) introduced only when required by evidence and explanatory necessity.

CCM is not a cognition ladder and not a scalar intelligence score. It is a comparative control architecture framework used to classify what kinds of regulation, persistence, anticipation, calibration, and later modelling must exist in order to explain different forms of organism–environment interaction.

This page explains the public conceptual role of CCM. It does not attempt to reproduce the full SCCA technical specification, pseudocode, or component-by-component execution contract.

CCM at a glance

This visual shows the core idea of CCM as a cumulative hierarchy. Higher capability layers are added on top of lower ones, while earlier control structures remain part of the organism’s operative architecture.

CLn axis

The vertical stack represents cumulative component levels: CL1, CL2, CL3, CL4, CLn, CLn+1. Each added layer reflects a new architectural commitment.

H — Temporal Horizon

H expresses how far ahead behaviour can be regulated in behaviour-class tick units. It captures future-relevant control depth, not human-style planning by default.

K — Structural Capacity

K expresses how much persistent internal structure must exist to support behaviour at that ceiling. It is about required architecture, not neuron counts or vague complexity.

Diagram note

The cube is a conceptual illustration. It does not imply linear or proportional scaling of CLn, H, or K. The visual simply represents the three dimensions considered when defining component boundaries.

What CCM explains

CCM describes consciousness in architectural terms. Rather than treating consciousness as a single property, it treats it as a progression of control capabilities that can be introduced incrementally and compared across life.

Each component boundary marks the point where a new architectural commitment becomes necessary to explain a behaviour or behavioural regime.

Within the broader framework, CM provides the organism-level viability-regulated control framing, while CCM provides the capability hierarchy used to classify that control in explicit, bounded, and comparable terms.

How to read the hierarchy

Layered architecture

CCM treats biological control as cumulative. Higher capabilities build on earlier mechanisms rather than replacing them, so a higher-ceiling organism can still exhibit lower-component behaviours where those remain sufficient.

The component language is written as CL1, CL2, CL3 … CLn, where each step introduces a distinct new control requirement.

Capability boundaries

Each boundary is introduced only when needed to explain behaviour. Typical diagnostic questions include whether behaviour requires:

  • persistence across control episodes
  • explicit threshold gating
  • history-dependent modulation
  • anticipatory timing or future-relevant control

What CCM is and is not

What it is
  • A cumulative hierarchy of bounded control capabilities
  • A comparative framework for classifying behaviour across life
  • A method for making architectural commitments explicit and testable
What it is not
  • Not a scalar measure of intelligence or consciousness magnitude
  • Not a synonym for awareness, reportability, or cognition
  • Not a complete theory of subjective experience by itself
