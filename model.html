Model

The Model section provides a structured map of the major conceptual parts of Consciousness Mechanics (CM).

Within this framework, CCM describes the cumulative hierarchy of control capabilities, while AoE describes the layered structure of experience grounded in global state. Together they sit within the broader CM architecture.

This section is organised into the two main model branches — Framework and Experience — followed by related pages on system behaviour and links to formal source material.

How to navigate the model

The model section is organised to make conceptual boundaries explicit. Each page explains one part of the framework, what it contributes, how it relates to neighbouring ideas, and what it does not claim.

Main branches
  • Framework — the overall architecture and classification logic
  • Experience — AoE, global state, and the layered structure of experience
  • System behaviour — perception, regulation, and action emerging within the wider CM framework
  • Formal material — papers, diagrams, and archived outputs
How to use this section
  • Use this page as the main index into the framework
  • Use the model navigation panel to move linearly or open the full map
  • Use concept pages to clarify scope and relation, not to replace the formal papers

That structure matters. One of the central aims of CM / CCM is to reduce conceptual conflation by separating distinct explanatory layers rather than collapsing them into a single vague account.
