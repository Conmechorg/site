Model

The Model section provides a structured map of the major conceptual parts of Consciousness Mechanics (CM) and the Consciousness Component Model (CCM).

It is designed as a public explanatory layer: a clear route into the framework, its major concepts, and their relations. The site pages are not a substitute for the papers. They are intended to make the architecture easier to navigate, understand, and evaluate.

Use this page as a conceptual index into the framework, experiential structure, organism-level control, and formal source material.

In this section

The model pages are grouped into a small number of conceptual branches.

  • Framework — the overall architecture and classification logic
  • Experience — the Architecture of Experience and its layers
  • Control — perception, regulation, and action within the wider system
  • Formal material — papers, diagrams, and archived outputs

Start with Architecture of Experience, or return to the CM overview.

How to use this page

The model section is organised to make conceptual boundaries explicit. Each page explains one part of the framework, what it contributes, how it relates to neighbouring ideas, and what it does not claim.

That structure matters. One of the central aims of CM / CCM is to reduce conceptual conflation by separating distinct explanatory layers rather than collapsing them into a single vague account.
