Approach

The Approach page explains the methodological rules used to build Consciousness Mechanics (CM) and the Consciousness Component Model (CCM).

The framework is developed under explicit constraints designed to prevent conceptual drift, reduce over-claim, and keep explanatory commitments tied to mechanism rather than metaphor.

These are not stylistic preferences. They are the rules that determine how components are introduced, how scope is bounded, and how the architecture remains comparable across life.

Why this approach exists

Consciousness research often suffers from conceptual drift. Terms such as awareness, experience, agency, and cognition are frequently used interchangeably, or defined only by informal description. That makes comparison across theories difficult and allows explanations to expand without clear architectural commitments.

CM / CCM is designed to avoid that problem. It adopts a disciplined methodological approach in which explanatory claims must correspond to identifiable control commitments. Rather than beginning with subjective phenomena and searching for mechanisms afterwards, the framework begins with organism-level control problems.

On this view, consciousness is not introduced as an additional property layered on top of biology. It is analysed as part of the control machinery through which living systems regulate their relationship with the environment while maintaining viability under evolutionary constraint.

Approach

The framework is developed under constraints designed to prevent conceptual drift and over-claim. These are rules of the build, not rhetorical guidelines. Each rule exists to keep explanations mechanistic, comparable across organisms, and resistant to narrative substitution.

Grounding & mechanism
  • Biology-first: define capabilities from whole-organism control problems.
  • Operational spec: components are expressed as variables, gates, and update rules.
  • No narrative substitution: explanation must cash out in mechanism.
Discipline & scope
  • Incremental ladder: add capability only when lower levels cannot explain the behaviour.
  • Conservative ceilings: absence of a component is a meaningful claim.
  • Scope boundaries: every component states what it does not claim.

The goal is a framework that stays falsifiable, extensible, and comparable across life, without importing human-specific assumptions as default explanations.

How these rules shape CM / CCM

These constraints directly shape the architecture of CM and CCM. Because explanations must resolve into mechanism, the framework is organised around explicit control components rather than descriptive themes alone.

Each component is expected to specify the variables it operates on, the gating conditions under which it activates, and the updates it performs on state. This is why the framework develops as a cumulative capability ladder: higher-level commitments are introduced only when lower-level mechanisms cannot account for the observed behaviour.

The same discipline also shapes the Architecture of Experience (AoE). Rather than treating experience as a single undifferentiated phenomenon, AoE describes how different experiential layers arise from perturbations of organism-level global state and how those layers relate to broader control architecture.

In this way, the approach is not separate from the model. It is the reason the model takes the form it does.

What this approach deliberately avoids

What it avoids
  • Emergence without architecture: appealing to emergence without specifying the mechanisms that generate it.
  • Narrative substitution: replacing mechanistic explanation with metaphor, imagery, or analogy.
  • Human-centric projection: starting from human cognition and projecting its structure downward by default.
Why that matters
  • Comparability: theories become easier to compare when claims are explicit.
  • Constraint: scope boundaries reduce inflation of explanatory commitments.
  • Clarity: distinct terms remain tied to distinct architectural roles.

Avoiding these patterns helps maintain a framework that remains mechanistic, falsifiable, and applicable across forms of life without collapsing into loose narrative description.

Why the site is organised this way

The site mirrors the same methodological discipline used to build the framework. Different pages are separated because they perform different explanatory jobs.

Method page
  • Approach: explains the rules used to build the framework.
  • Model: shows the resulting architecture and conceptual map.
Architecture pages
  • CM: explains the organism-level control framing.
  • CCM: explains the cumulative capability hierarchy.
  • AoE: explains the layered structure of experience.

This separation is intentional. One of the central aims of CM / CCM is to reduce conceptual conflation by distinguishing explanatory layers rather than collapsing them into a single vague account.
