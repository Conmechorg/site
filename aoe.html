Architecture of
Experience

The Architecture of Experience (AoE) explains how experience is structured within Consciousness Mechanics (CM).

It describes how viability-relevant signals perturb an organism’s global state (GS), and how layered forms of experience arise from that process: feeling, valence, awareness, cognition, and emotion.

AoE addresses experiential structure, not the entire control system. Broader functions such as agency, action selection, and downstream behavioural organisation belong to the wider CM / CCM architecture.

What AoE explains

The Architecture of Experience provides a layered account of experience grounded in organism-level control.

In this model, experience is not treated as an isolated mental substance or as a free-floating property detached from biological function. Instead, experience is understood as arising from changes in the organism’s global state as it regulates itself in relation to the environment.

AoE separates the experiential structure into distinct layers so that terms often conflated in consciousness discussions can be treated more precisely. At the centre of the model is a simple claim: experience is structured by how organism-relevant signals perturb global state, and by how those perturbations are subsequently processed.

The layered structure of experience

Feeling

Feeling is the most basic experiential layer in the model.

It corresponds to the occurrence of a perturbation in global state. When organism-relevant input changes the condition of the system in a way that matters to viability, that perturbation is felt. Feeling is not yet a judgement, concept, or narrative. It is the immediate experiential aspect of organism-level state disturbance.

Valence

Valence is the directional significance of that perturbation relative to the organism’s viability.

It reflects whether the change in global state is tending in a beneficial, harmful, relieving, destabilising, or otherwise viability-relevant direction. Within AoE, valence is not an optional add-on to feeling. It is a core part of how feeling is structured in a living control system.

Awareness

Awareness is the selective registration of viability-relevant signals for further processing.

Not every signal affecting the organism enters awareness in the same way. AoE treats awareness as a more structured layer in which certain perturbations, or the signals associated with them, become available for coordinated evaluation within the system. Awareness is about what is brought into experiential availability, not yet the full interpretive machinery that may follow.

Cognition

Cognition is the layer in which experience becomes integrated into modelling, interpretation, comparison, and structured evaluation.

This includes processes such as categorisation, recall, inference, and the use of internal models to interpret current conditions. In AoE, cognition is not the source of all experience. It is a higher layer that operates on already structured experiential material.

Emotion

Emotion is the higher-order experiential configuration that emerges when feeling, valence, awareness, and cognition interact in organised ways.

In this framework, emotion is not reducible to bare feeling alone, nor to cognition alone. It depends on the way state perturbation, viability significance, selective awareness, and interpretive processing are combined. AoE therefore treats emotion as a structured experiential outcome built from lower layers, rather than as a primitive indivisible category.

Sentience and qualitative experience

Within AoE, sentient experience is most closely associated with:

{ Feeling, Valence }

This captures the idea that sentience begins where organism-level perturbation is not only occurring, but occurring with experienced viability significance.

On this view, sentience is not identical to full cognition, self-reflection, or language. It marks the presence of felt and valenced experience.

AoE also distinguishes a broader class of qualitative experience, which can be approximated as:

{ Feeling, Valence, Awareness, Emotion }

The aim is not to impose arbitrary labels, but to provide a clearer mechanistic structure for terms that are often used loosely or interchangeably.

Scope and boundaries

AoE explains the structure of experience within the CM framework. It does not attempt to describe the entire control architecture of the organism.

What AoE is about
  • Experiential organisation within a living system
  • Perturbation of global state
  • Viability-relevant signal processing as the basis of perception
  • The layered relation between feeling, valence, awareness, cognition, and emotion
What belongs to broader CM / CCM
  • Agency and action selection
  • Downstream behavioural organisation
  • Policy, control, and wider system architecture
  • The cumulative capability hierarchy defined by CCM

This distinction matters. The Architecture of Experience is one part of the larger framework: it explains how experience is structured, while the broader model explains how conscious systems regulate, model, and act.
