Consciousness Mechanics (CM)

Consciousness Mechanics (CM) is a biology-first research framework that models consciousness as a continuous control architecture through which organisms perceive change, evaluate its significance for viability, interact with their environment, and regulate their ongoing condition.

The framework includes CCM as the comparative capability hierarchy and AoE as the experiential branch within that broader control architecture.

Core Definition Of Consciousness

Consciousness is the mechanism by which an organism regulates viability through perception and interaction with its environment.

CM begins from a direct biological claim. Consciousness is not a late add-on, a mysterious extra property, or a human-only phenomenon. It is the organism-level control architecture through which living systems perceive, evaluate, and act in ways that sustain ongoing viability.

On this view, consciousness is not layered on top of life after sufficient complexity appears. It is part of the control logic through which an organism maintains itself in the face of change.
