Consciousness Mechanics (CM)

Consciousness is the mechanism by which an organism regulates viability through perception and interaction with its environment.

Consciousness Mechanics (CM) is a biology-first research framework that models consciousness as a continuous control architecture through which organisms perceive change, evaluate its significance for viability, interact with their environment, and regulate their ongoing condition.

The wider framework includes CCM as the formal comparative capability hierarchy and AoE as the experiential branch structured within that broader control architecture.

Core definition

CM begins from a simple but explicit claim: consciousness is the mechanism by which an organism regulates viability through perception and interaction with its environment.

On this view, consciousness is not a late add-on, a mysterious extra property, or a human-only phenomenon. It is the organism-level control architecture within which this mechanism operates, enabling living systems to perceive, evaluate, and act in ways that sustain ongoing viability.

This site is the public conceptual map of that framework, alongside the formal papers and diagrams.

The CM control loop

The CM control loop is the clearest entry point into the framework. It shows consciousness as a continuous loop in which an organism perceives change, evaluates its significance for viability, selects a response, acts, and updates its condition through interaction with its environment.

The same loop scales across increasing capability, from reflex through regulation, awareness, cognition, and planning, without introducing a separate mechanism.

Overview

CM is the public home of an in-progress research framework, built to be explicit, conservative, and mechanistically checkable. It is not a finished theory, and not a position statement.

What this site will publish
  • Component definitions that behave like engineering specs, including state, gates, and update rules.
  • Diagrams and pseudocode that show how control capability scales across organisms.
  • Anchors and discriminators showing what would count as evidence for or against a capability.
What it will avoid
  • Metaphor-led explanations that cannot be operationalised.
  • Assuming higher cognition by default and projecting it downward.
  • Over-claiming beyond the defined scope of each component.

If you’re evaluating whether CCM has substance, the fastest indicator is not prose. It is whether each component can be read as a bounded mechanism with a clear ceiling, and whether the ladder remains coherent as it climbs.

Approach

The framework is developed under constraints designed to prevent conceptual drift and over-claim. These are “rules of the build”, not rhetoric.

Grounding & mechanism
  • Biology-first: define capabilities from whole-organism control problems.
  • Operational spec: components are expressed as variables, gates, and update rules.
  • No narrative substitution: explanation must cash out in mechanism.
Discipline & scope
  • Incremental ladder: add capability only when lower levels cannot explain the behaviour.
  • Conservative ceilings: absence of a component is a meaningful claim.
  • Scope boundaries: every component states what it does not claim.

The goal is a framework that stays falsifiable, extensible, and comparable across life, without importing human-specific assumptions as default explanations.
