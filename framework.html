Framework

The Framework branch explains the broader architectural and comparative structure of Consciousness Mechanics (CM).

It includes the organism-level framing of CM, the cumulative capability hierarchy of CCM, and the comparative axes used to keep temporal and structural claims explicit: the H Scale and K Scale.

This branch is about architectural scope, classification logic, and bounded control commitments. It is not the experiential branch of the site, and it does not by itself explain the full structure of experience.

What this branch covers

The Framework branch gathers the pages that define the broader architectural and comparative structure of the model. These pages explain what CM is claiming at the level of organismal control, how CCM formalises cumulative capability, and how the H and K scales help keep temporal and structural commitments explicit.

This part of the site is primarily about architecture, classification, and disciplined scope. It sets out the explanatory skeleton within which later branches such as Experience and System behaviour can be understood.

How the framework pages relate

Core structure
  • CM provides the umbrella organism-level framing
  • CCM specifies cumulative capability boundaries within that framing
  • H makes temporal horizon commitments explicit
  • K makes structural capacity commitments explicit
Why this matters
  • It prevents vague appeals to emergence without mechanism
  • It supports comparison across life without anthropomorphic assumptions
  • It separates architectural claims from experiential claims

Boundary of this branch

The Framework branch does not attempt to explain the whole of subjective or experiential structure on its own. Those issues are treated in the Experience branch, especially through AoE and the pages that sit beneath it.

Nor is this branch mainly about behavioural output at the whole-organism level. Questions about perception, regulation, and action across the wider system sit more naturally under System behaviour.
